The right mileage tracking app can save you thousands of dollars a year in tax deductions — but the wrong one can cost you time, privacy, and money. We tested six of the most popular mileage trackers available in 2026 and ranked them on the factors that matter most to self-employed professionals: accuracy, pricing, IRS/CRA compliance, battery impact, privacy, and how much work they actually save you.

Disclosure: This comparison is published by MyMilesAI. We've done our best to represent competitors fairly — our users deserve an honest picture.

What to Look for in a Mileage Tracking App

Before diving into individual apps, here's what actually matters:

  • Automatic trip detection: Does it start logging without you opening the app?
  • GPS accuracy: Within 2–3% of actual mileage is IRS-acceptable.
  • IRS/CRA compliance: Does the exported report include date, destination, purpose, and miles?
  • Pricing: Monthly cost relative to the deduction value it helps you capture.
  • Battery drain: Constant GPS background usage can kill your battery by 15–20%.
  • Data privacy: Does the app upload your location data to their servers?
  • Ease of classification: How quickly can you mark a trip as business or personal?

MileIQ

MileIQ (Microsoft)
$9.99/month · $5.99/mo annual
MileIQ is the most widely known mileage tracker, now owned by Microsoft. It pioneered the swipe-to-classify interface and has a large user base. The app automatically detects drives and presents them as cards in a feed — swipe right for business, left for personal.
Pros
  • Reliable auto-detection
  • Clean, familiar UI
  • Microsoft 365 integration
  • Good export options (CSV, PDF)
Cons
  • $9.99/month is among the most expensive
  • All location data uploaded to Microsoft servers
  • No AI auto-classification — every trip requires manual swipe
  • No Canada (CRA) support
  • Requires internet to function

Everlance

Everlance
$10/month · $8/mo annual
Everlance positions itself as the premium option, adding expense tracking alongside mileage. If you want a combined mileage + expense tracker, it has more features than most. However, that breadth comes with complexity and a higher price tag.
Pros
  • Expense tracking built in
  • Team / fleet features
  • Good accountant export
  • Some AI classification
Cons
  • Most expensive option at $10/mo
  • Complex UI — more features than most drivers need
  • Location data sent to servers
  • Battery drain complaints from users
  • Limited CRA support

TripLog

TripLog
$5.99/month · Free tier available
TripLog is a solid mid-range option with good IRS and CRA compliance features, making it one of the few apps that genuinely works for Canadian users. It supports multiple vehicles, which is useful for professionals who split time between a personal car and a work truck.
Pros
  • Lower price point ($5.99/mo)
  • Strong CRA + IRS compliance
  • Multi-vehicle support
  • Free tier with 40 trips/month
Cons
  • No AI classification — manual categorization required
  • UI feels dated compared to newer apps
  • Cloud-based data storage
  • Customer support can be slow

Driversnote

Driversnote
$9.99/month · $6.99/mo annual
Driversnote is a Danish company with a strong reputation in Europe and growing North American presence. It supports iBeacon — a Bluetooth-based trigger that automatically starts and stops tracking when you get in and out of your car. For users whose phones don't always reliably auto-detect drives via motion sensors, this is a meaningful differentiator.
Pros
  • iBeacon Bluetooth auto-start
  • Good mileage log reports
  • IRS and CRA support
  • Team features
Cons
  • $9.99/mo — high for individual users
  • Requires additional Bluetooth device for best accuracy
  • No AI classification
  • Cloud data storage

Hurdlr

Hurdlr
$8.34/month (annual) · Free tier
Hurdlr combines mileage tracking with income tracking, expense management, and estimated tax calculation — making it a lightweight alternative to a full accounting app for freelancers. If you want a single app that tracks miles AND estimates your quarterly taxes, Hurdlr is worth considering. Pure mileage trackers will beat it on GPS accuracy and trip management, though.
Pros
  • Built-in quarterly tax estimator
  • Income + expense tracking
  • Free tier available
  • Good for gig workers needing full financial picture
Cons
  • Mileage tracking is secondary to its core focus
  • US only — no CRA support
  • No AI classification
  • Less accurate GPS than dedicated trackers
